About me.

I'm Cinerita — a mechanical engineer turned additive manufacturing specialist, speaker, and maker. I graduated from NYU Tandon with an MS in Mechanical Engineering in 2024. I love building things that didn't exist before, giving talks about how we make them, and finding the overlap between precision engineering and genuine creativity.

Background

I've spent the last few years at the intersection of mechanical engineering and manufacturing — from building a NASA competition robot at NYU to managing a 10,000 sq ft makerspace. I'm drawn to projects where you can hold the output in your hands.

NYU Robotics Design Team — NASA Lunabotics
Manufacturing Lead · Systems Engineer · Project Manager · 2022–2024
The NYU Robotics Design Team competed in NASA's annual Lunabotics Competition, designing and engineering an extraterrestrial mining robot. As Manufacturing Lead I led a team of 15 to manufacture the locomotion system — spoked wheels with 3D printed rims, steel spokes, hex shaft motor connections, and an aluminium box tube chassis. As Project Manager I managed a $40,500 budget across 60 team members spanning electrical, mechanical, and software. As Systems Engineer I ran design reviews and collaborated across teams to meet NASA requirements.

NYU Tandon MakerSpace
Graduate Assistant · MakerGarage · 2023–2024
The NYU Tandon MakerSpace is a 10,000 sq ft cutting-edge lab at the intersection of technology, creativity, and innovation. As Graduate Assistant for the MakerGarage I managed the space, maintained 3D printers, laser cutters, and 3D scanners, and trained students to use machines safely. Key projects included setting up the UR16e robotic arm programme, learning and documenting the Einscan SE and Artec Eva 3D scanners, and leading a team of 3 to assemble the Marathon Systems 52f spray booth.

NASA Leaps and Bounds Award
NASA Lunabotics 2024 · Systems Engineering Award
Presented to New York University at the NASA Lunabotics 2024 competition. The Leaps and Bounds award is given to the team that demonstrated the most significant progress since the previous year. NASA also requires every team to submit a systems engineering paper detailing the planning, design and construction of the robot.

100% on NASA Project Management Plan
NASA Lunabotics 2024
As project manager I authored the Project Management Plan required by NASA before the start of the competition. This included budget estimation, schedule planning, and risk management across three competencies and 60 team members. The plan was submitted to NASA and received a perfect 100% grade.
